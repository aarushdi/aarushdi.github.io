Biography

Thank you for visiting my webpage. I am a research scientist, interested in the broad areas of signal processing, uncertainty quantification, and machine learning. Currently, I am a Research Initiatives Manager with Stanford University’s Institute for Human-Centered Artificial Intelligence (HAI), working with diverse AI communities (e.g., ML/DL, XAI, HCI, NLP, and CV) across Stanford and the corporate world, in order to envision, build, and maintain new bridges around cutting-edge research for a variety of domain applications.

Prior to joining Stanford, I was a research scientist at the Center for Computing Research of Sandia National Laboratories, where I worked with the Dakota team and PI'd a number of projects on UQ/ML. Before Sandia, I was an R&D manager of data science at Northrop Grumman Corporation, a research fellow at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering of UC Davis and the Computational Visualization Center under Oden Institute for Computational Engineering and Sciences at UT Austin, and an R&D engineer at Cisco Systems.

I hold a Ph.D. degree in Electrical and Computer Engineering (with a minor in Applied Math) from the University of California, Davis, and MS.c./BS.c. degrees in Electronics and Electrical Communications Engineering from Cairo University, Egypt. I am a senior member of IEEE and a member of ACM and SIAM.

Please feel free to reach out if you have any questions!
